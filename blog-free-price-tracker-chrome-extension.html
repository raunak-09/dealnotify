The Friction of Manual Price Tracking

Price tracking is one of those ideas everyone agrees with and almost nobody actually does. The logic is airtight — prices move constantly, and waiting a few weeks can save you real money — but the effort per product is just high enough to kill the habit. You find something you like, tell yourself you'll set an alert later, and by the time "later" comes around, you've either bought it at full price or forgotten entirely.

The issue has never been the monitoring. DealNotify has been watching prices on Amazon, Walmart, Best Buy, Target, eBay and Costco around the clock for a long time. The issue has been everything you had to do before the monitoring started: the copy-paste, the tab switch, the re-entry of information the product page already contained.

"The best price tracker is the one you actually use. And you'll only use it if it's already where you're shopping."

— DealNotify Team

Meet the DealNotify Chrome Extension

The extension lives quietly in your Chrome toolbar. When you visit a product page on a supported retailer, the DealNotify icon lights up with a small green dot — that's your signal that the page is trackable. Click it once, and a clean popup slides open with everything already filled in: the product name, the image, and the current price, pulled directly from the page you're looking at.

From there, you can:

  • Choose your alert type — Price Drop (set a target price) or Restock (get notified the moment a sold-out item is available again).
  • Set your target in seconds — a single field, one submit button. No second tab, no re-entering the product URL.
  • Track across retailers seamlessly — the same extension works on Amazon, Walmart, Best Buy, Target, eBay and Costco. One install, six stores.
  • Manage everything from your dashboard — every product you add flows straight into your DealNotify account, where you can edit targets, pause alerts, or review your tracking history in one place.

Why an Extension — and Why Now?

If you've read our other posts, you've seen us argue that browser-only price trackers are a weaker model than server-side monitoring. That's still true. Extensions that only check prices when the tab is open will miss every overnight flash sale, every 3am restock, every mid-week price adjustment that happens when you're not looking. You can't out-refresh an algorithm that updates 2.5 million prices a day.

So the DealNotify extension isn't a tracker. It's a one-click input layer for the tracker. All of the actual monitoring still happens exactly where it should — on our servers, continuously, whether or not you're online. The extension just removes the one thing shoppers hated: the setup step.

Think of it as the best of both worlds. Browser-level convenience when you're adding something to track. Cloud-level reliability once you've added it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the DealNotify Chrome extension free? +

Yes. The extension is free to install, and the free plan lets you track up to 3 products with full price-drop and restock alert functionality. No credit card required. If you need to track more, Pro is $4.99/month for unlimited products.

Which retailers does the extension support? +

Amazon (.com, .co.uk and .ca), Walmart, Best Buy, Target, eBay and Costco. The extension automatically recognises product pages on any of these sites and lets you start tracking in one click.

Does it work on Microsoft Edge, Brave or Opera? +

Yes. DealNotify is a Chromium extension, so it works on any Chromium-based browser — Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Brave, Opera, Arc, and others. Install it from the Chrome Web Store and it will run on all of them.

Will tracking stop if I close my browser? +

No. Once you've added a product, DealNotify monitors it on our servers 24/7 — not in your browser. You can close every tab, shut down your laptop, even uninstall the extension, and the tracking carries on. The extension is just a faster way to add new products.

What data does the extension access? +

Only the product page you're actively viewing on one of the six supported retailers. The extension uses just two permissions — activeTab and storage — and does not read your browsing history, your other tabs, or any site outside the supported retailer list.
